    How this table is calculated

    Average salience measures how strongly parties focus on a topic overall. For each topic, the table looks at every party score and measures how far it sits from the neutral midpoint of 3. Those distances are then averaged across all parties. Topics with higher average salience are shown higher up the table because parties take clearer and more differentiated positions on them.

    Party Top Focus marks the single topic where a party has its highest score in the current manifesto data. Party Least Focus marks the single topic where a party has its lowest score. If several topics tie for the same highest or lowest score, the table uses the one with the higher overall average salience as the tiebreak.
