    How We Calculate

    The matcher uses a transparent scoring model based on your chosen topics, your preferred direction on each issue, and how strongly you care about it.

    1. Topic selection and position setting

    You select up to 10 topics, then set both direction and strength on each one. Direction becomes a sign value, and strength becomes a user weighting score.

    USS = user strength score (1 to 5) UserSign = support → +1 UserSign = neutral → 0 UserSign = oppose → -1

    2. Party scoring scale

    Each party gets a topic score from 1 to 5. These scores represent how strongly the party supports or opposes a policy area, based on manifesto data where published, or website and campaign material where not.

    5 = strongly champions 4 = supportive 3 = neutral / no clear position 2 = mild opposition 1 = strongly opposed

    3. Match contribution formula

    For each selected topic, the model calculates a match contribution using the party score, your chosen direction, and your strength. Positive values increase a party's total, and negative values decrease it.

    MC = USS × (PS - 3) × UserSign Worked example: USS = 5 PS = 5 UserSign = +1 MC = 5 × (5 - 3) × 1 = 10

    4. Normalisation to 0–100

    Each party's total PMS is then normalised using min-max scaling so the highest-scoring party appears at 100 and the lowest at 0. If all scores are equal, every party is shown as 50.

    PMS[P] += MC mx = Math.max(...PMS values) mn = Math.min(...PMS values) range = mx - mn if range <= 0: norm[P] = 50 else: norm[P] = Math.round((PMS[P] - mn) / range × 100)

    5. Limitations and transparency

    The site is non-partisan and shows its scoring method publicly. Scores are only as current as the underlying manifesto data and published sources. Where a party has not published a manifesto, its score is labelled as estimated and derived from public website and campaign material.

    When a published manifesto differs meaningfully from website messaging on a topic you selected, the results page flags that discrepancy separately so the scoring source remains visible.
